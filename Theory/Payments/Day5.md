## Day 5: Who Built This? (Behind the Curtain)

Welcome to Day 5! Today, we’re looking behind the curtain to see **who actually runs the show.**

In an interview, people often use **"SWIFT"** and **"ISO 20022"** as if they are the same thing.

They aren't!

Understanding the difference will make you look very sharp.

---

### 🏗️ 2.4: Who Developed ISO 20022?

ISO 20022 wasn't built by one company. It was built by a **community of financial experts from all over the world.**

Think of it like a **World Parliament for Money.**

The main groups are:

### RMG (Registration Management Group)

These are the **"Big Bosses."**

- They are the highest governing body.
- They are made up of senior people from banks and financial groups.
- They decide the overall strategy of ISO 20022.
- They approve major changes and oversee governance.

### SEGs (Standards Evaluation Groups)

These are the **"Subject Experts."**

- There is a SEG for:
  - Payments
  - Securities (stocks)
  - Foreign Exchange
- They review and evaluate every new message definition.
- They ensure that proposed messages:
  - Actually solve real industry problems
  - Are technically sound
  - Can be implemented by financial institutions

They act as quality control for the entire ecosystem.

---

## 📮 2.5: SWIFT's Role in ISO 20022

This is the most important part of today.

SWIFT is the **"Registration Authority" (RA)** for ISO 20022.

To understand this, let's use a **library analogy:**

- **ISO** is the organization that decided how books should be written (the rules).
- **SWIFT** is the **Librarian.**

SWIFT does not "own" the language.

But SWIFT:
- Keeps the official "dictionary"
- Updates the "books"
- Maintains the data repository
- Hosts the official website (iso20022.org)
- Publishes and maintains message definitions

In simple terms:

> ISO sets the rules.  
> SWIFT maintains and operationalizes them.

---

## The Network vs. The Standard

In your interview, **keep these two completely separate:**

### SWIFT = The Network

- The secure global messaging network
- The "secure pipes" that banks use to send messages
- Infrastructure provider

### ISO 20022 = The Standard

- The financial messaging language
- The structured format of the message
- The content spoken inside the pipes

---

### Key Fact (Very Important for Interviews)

You can use the **ISO 20022 language without using the SWIFT network.**

For example:
- A local country’s clearing system can adopt ISO 20022.
- A domestic real-time payment system can use ISO 20022.
- Central banks can implement ISO 20022 internally.

However:

> SWIFT is the biggest global driver pushing adoption of ISO 20022 for cross-border payments.

---

## 💼 The "Interview Answer"

If they ask:

**"Is ISO 20022 owned by SWIFT?"**

You can answer:

> "No, ISO 20022 is an open global standard managed by the International Organization for Standardization. However, SWIFT plays a critical role as the Registration Authority (RA). They maintain the data dictionary and the business process catalogue. Essentially, while ISO sets the rules, SWIFT acts as the custodian that ensures the standard is implemented correctly across the global financial community."

This answer shows:
- Governance understanding
- Industry awareness
- Clear separation of responsibilities
