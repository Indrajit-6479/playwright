You've mastered the **"messages"** (the talk), but now we have to talk about the **"money"** (the walk).

---

## 🤝 5.1: What is Correspondent Banking?

> **The Village Analogy:**
> Imagine you live in a small village and want to send a gift to someone in a distant city. You don't have a car, but your neighbor does — and he knows someone in that city. You give the gift to your neighbor, he drives it halfway, and hands it to his friend who finishes the delivery.
>
> **Correspondent Banking is exactly that.**

It is a **network of banks that provide services to each other.**

- Most banks do **not** have branches in every country.
- To move money to a country where they don't have a branch, they use a **Correspondent Bank** — a "partner" bank in that country — to act as their **bridge.**

### How It Works

```
Bank A (India)                                          Bank B (USA)
[Doesn't have a US branch]        ───────────────►    [Acts as Correspondent]
                                  Correspondent        [Holds USD on behalf
                                  Relationship         of Bank A]
```

## 📔 5.2: Nostro, Vostro, and Loro Accounts

> These three terms are the **"Latin" of banking.** They describe the **same account** but from **different perspectives.**
> If you get these right in an interview, you'll sound like you've worked in a bank for years.

---

### The Perspective Shift

> Think of these as **"Our money," "Your money,"** and **"Their money."**

| Term       | Latin Meaning | Simple English Definition  | The "Banker" View                                                     |
| ---------- | ------------- | -------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Nostro** | *"Ours"*      | Our money held **by** you. | Bank A has a USD account **at** Bank B in New York.                   |
| **Vostro** | *"Yours"*     | Your money held **by** us. | Bank B sees the account Bank A opened as a **Vostro** account.        |
| **Loro**   | *"Theirs"*    | Their money held by you.   | Bank C (an outsider) refers to the account between Bank A and Bank B. |

---

### 💡 The Memory Hack

- **Nostro = "Our"** — Think: *"**N**ostro = **N**ot theirs, it's **OURS**"*
- **Vostro = "Your"** — Think: *"**V**ostro = the **V**ault belongs to **YOU**"*
- **Loro = "Their"** — Think: *"**L**oro = **L**ooks like it belongs to someone **e**Lse"*

---

### The Classic Scenario

> **HDFC Bank (India)** opens an account at **JP Morgan (USA)** to hold US Dollars.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐     ┌──────────────────────────────────┐
│         HDFC Bank               │     │          JP Morgan               │
│         (India)                 │     │          (USA)                   │
│                                 │     │                                  │
│  "We have a USD account         │     │  "HDFC has opened an account     │
│   at JP Morgan."                │     │   with us."                      │
│                                 │     │                                  │
│  ► Calls it their               │     │  ► Calls it HDFC's               │
│    NOSTRO account               │     │    VOSTRO account                │
└─────────────────────────────────┘     └──────────────────────────────────┘
                    │                                   │
                    └──────── SAME ACCOUNT ─────────────┘
                         (just seen from two sides)
```

> **It is the exact same account — just seen from two different sides of the ocean.**

---

## 💼 The "Interview Answer"

**Q: "Can you explain the difference between a Nostro and a Vostro account?"**

> **A:** *"They are actually **two sides of the same coin.** A **Nostro account** refers to 'our' money held by another bank in a foreign currency — for example, a UK bank holding USD at a US bank. That same account, from the perspective of the US bank, is a **Vostro account**, meaning 'your money held by us.' These accounts are the essential **'ledger'** that allows correspondent banking to function **without the physical movement of cash.**"*

---

## 🎯 Day 17 Key Takeaways

1. **Correspondent Banking** — A partnership where banks provide services for each other in different countries
2. **Nostro** — An account a bank holds **at** another bank *(usually in a foreign country/currency)*
3. **Vostro** — An account another bank holds **at your** bank
4. **Mirroring** — For every Nostro account in the world, there is a corresponding Vostro account on the other bank's books
5. **Loro** — How a **third-party** bank refers to an account between two other banks
