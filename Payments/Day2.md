### Day 2: From the "What" to the "Why"

Welcome to **Day 2**! Today, we’re moving from the **"What"** (the map) to the **"Why."**

If you're going to work for a multinational bank, you'll hear the word **ISO** a thousand times a day. Think of today as learning why everyone in the world finally agreed to use the same "plug" for their electrical outlets—but for money.

---

### 🏗️ 2.1: What is ISO? (The Foundation of Global Standards)

**ISO** stands for the **International Organization for Standardization**.  

- Based in Switzerland, ISO doesn't make products; they make **rules**.  
- They decide things like:
  - A "second" is a second
  - An A4 piece of paper is a specific size
  - Credit cards should be the same shape so they fit into every ATM in the world

### Why does a Bank care about ISO?

Imagine:

- Bank A sends a message: `"Send one hundred dollars"`  
- Bank B only understands: `"Transfer 100 USD"`

**Without Standards:**  
- A human would have to manually fix the message  
- Slow, expensive, prone to errors  

**With Standards:**  
- Computers talk to each other instantly  
- Both banks follow the same **ISO rulebook**  

---

### The "Banker Talk" Vocabulary

Use these terms in your interview to sound professional:

| Professional Term                     | What it actually means                                                                 |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Interoperability**                  | The ability of different bank systems to "talk" to each other without a translator     |
| **STP (Straight-Through Processing)** | Payments that go from start to finish automatically without a human ever touching them |
| **Harmonization**                     | Getting every country’s banking system to sing from the same songbook                  |

---

### Why ISO 20022 is the "Gold Standard"

Before **ISO 20022**, banks used **ISO 15022** (which used "MT" messages).  

- ISO 15022 was like sending a text message with a **character limit**.  
- It worked, but it was "thin"—you couldn't include much detail.  

**ISO 20022** is "thick." It allows for:

- **More Data**: Include ultimate sender, invoice number, tax details  
- **Better Security**: Harder for criminals to hide money with detailed data  
- **Less "False Positives"**: High-quality data reduces accidental fraud alerts  

**Pro-Tip for the Interview:**  
If asked, `"Why is the bank moving to ISO 20022?"`  
Answer:  
> "To improve STP rates, enhance data quality for regulatory compliance, and ensure global interoperability."

#### 🎯 Day 2 Key Takeaways

- **ISO** is the global "Rule Maker" headquartered in Switzerland  
- **Standards** are necessary because they allow for **Straight-Through Processing (STP)** — money moves faster and cheaper  
- **ISO 20022** is the specific **"Financial Language"** we are moving toward because it carries much richer data than old standards
