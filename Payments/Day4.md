# Day 4: The Calendar

Welcome to Day 4! Today, we are looking at the **"Calendar."**

In an interview, they might ask you:  
*"Why did the bank spend so much money on this change now?"*  

To answer that, you need to understand that this wasn't a quick update—it was a **20-year journey** that just reached its biggest milestone.

---

## ⏳ 2.3: The ISO 20022 Timeline (2004–2025 and Beyond)

Even though your notes say 2025, since we are now in early 2026, we are living in the **"Post-Migration" era.** The world of payments has officially changed.

### The 20-Year Evolution

Think of this like moving an entire city from one island to another while everyone is still trying to go to work.  
You **can't just shut down the banks for a weekend** to do it.

- **2004 – The Birth:** ISO 20022 was officially published. At first, it was just an idea.  
- **2008–2014 – The SEPA Era:** Europe was the "guinea pig." They started using ISO 20022 for domestic Euro payments (SEPA). It worked!  
- **2018–2022 – The Planning:** Global banks and SWIFT realized the old MT (Message Text) system couldn't keep up with modern data needs (like anti-money laundering rules).  
- **March 2023 – The "Big Bang":** This was the start of the Coexistence Period. For nearly three years, banks had to speak both the old language (MT) and the new language (MX) at the same time.  
- **November 2025 – The Finish Line:** This was the official deadline for SWIFT cross-border payments. Banks were expected to have moved their main traffic to ISO 20022.  

---

## The "Coexistence" Concept (Critical for Interviews)

During the last few years, the biggest challenge for banks was **Coexistence**.

- Imagine you speak English (MX), but your friend only speaks French (MT). To talk, you need a **Translator.**  
- Banks used **Translation Services** to convert messages back and forth.  

**The Risk:** Data "truncation."  
- If an MX message has a 140-character address but the MT message only has 35 characters, the extra data gets **"cut off."**  
- This is a nightmare for compliance teams!

---

## 💼 The "Interview Answer"

If they ask: *"What was the significance of the November 2025 deadline?"*  

> "November 2025 marked the end of the SWIFT coexistence period for cross-border payments.  
> It was the point where the industry moved away from legacy MT messages for core payment clearing and fully embraced the rich data of ISO 20022.  
> As of 2026, the focus has shifted from 'migration' to 'data utilization'—actually using all that extra info to improve customer service and fraud detection."

---

## 🎯 Day 4 Key Takeaways

- **It wasn't overnight:** It took over 20 years to get the whole world to agree.  
- **Coexistence was the hard part:** Managing two languages at once led to "data truncation" risks.  
- **We are "Live":** As of today (2026), ISO 20022 is no longer the "future"—it is the **current standard.**  

You've mastered the history! Now you know why your role in a bank today is so important.
